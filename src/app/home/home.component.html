Apache Groovy Diversity Initiative

♦

Building a more diverse and more inclusive Groovy community

The groovy diversity initiative is an organisation aimed at increasing diversity with the Apache Groovy programming language community. Increasing diversity can mean many things. Specifically, we hope to increase number of people from underrepresented minorities in our community including, but not limited to gender diversity, racial/ethnic diversity, and socioeconomic diversity. The main ways in which we seek to increase diversity are:

Education

Job Opportunities

Networking

History

♦

GR8DI is an organization founded in 2017 by former GR8Ladies co-founder Jennifer Strater (aka codeJENNerator) and Kamwa Fosso Arcel Raulain (aka kraulain) of the University of Buea in Cameroon. In 2013, GR8Ladies was founded to support women in the Apache Groovy community, but as time passed, we wanted to move to a more open community that supports not just women but other underrepresented minorities as well. For a little background on GR8Ladies, please see the 2014 GR8Ladies Mission blog post.
